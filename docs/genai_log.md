# GenAI Transparency Log — Team DataX

This log documents how, where, and why our team used generative AI tools during the build. It demonstrates **critical evaluation** of AI outputs — every entry has a specific prompt, exact output usage, and a validation step. Where we rejected or modified AI suggestions, the reason is documented.

**Bottom line:** The team chose the problem framing, the methodology family, the cohort structure, the validation strategy, the business modules, the web-app layout, and the limitations narrative. AI tooling was used to write boilerplate, surface references, debug stack traces, draft scaffolding, and accelerate the parts that did not need original thought. Every output was reviewed against the raw data, the rubric, or domain knowledge before it landed in the repository.

**How to read this log:**
- Section "What the Team Decided (Not the AI)" lists the substantive design choices.
- Section "Log Entries" is the full audit trail of AI interactions in build order.
- Each entry records the prompt category, the AI output, the team action (accepted as-is / accepted with caveat / rejected), and the validation step taken.

## What the Team Decided (Not the AI)

This is the substantive intellectual content of the submission. Every choice below was reasoned through by the team using the problem statement, the rubric, and domain knowledge of FMCG retail in Sri Lanka. The AI's role afterwards was to implement these decisions in code — not to author them.

### Problem framing and methodology family

- **Reading the problem statement.** We re-read sections 1-6 of Problem.md three times before writing a line of code. We extracted the key statement "you are not given a target variable (y)" and immediately ruled out supervised ML — training on the censored historical V would bake constraint pattern into the model and reproduce censored values rather than uncap them. We also extracted the exact `V = min(D, C)` framing for our framework.
- **Choice of statistical-framework family.** We chose a censored / frontier estimation family of methods over ML because the problem matches the textbook setup (Tobin 1958, Aigner et al 1977). We picked peer-conditional Q90 as the primary anchor (non-parametric, defensible), then added three independent parametric cross-checks (log-linear, Tobit MLE, SFA) so the final result rests on convergence rather than a single model.
- **Architecture choice.** Bronze → Silver → Gold lakehouse with a documented quarantine store. This is the literal rubric line in §4.1 (40% weight); we matched it deliberately.

### Cohort and constraint design

- **Peer cohort definition.** `Outlet_Type × Outlet_Size × Province × POI-density-tier`. We chose these four axes from FMCG-domain reasoning: type drives consumption pattern, size drives capacity, province drives macro demand, POI tier drives micro catchment. Within-province POI-tier normalisation was our idea to mitigate OSM rural-coverage bias.
- **Hierarchical fallback levels.** L0 (full) → L1 (drop POI tier) → L2 (drop province) → L3 (drop size) → L4 (global). We chose this nesting because each level should remove the least-important axis first. Required minimum cohort size of 30 was a team judgment about quantile stability.
- **Constraint detection rules.** Four-rule disjunction designed by the team: (1) stockout sandwich (zero between non-zero), (2) zero in an outlet that has any positive month (intermittent supply), (3) Cooler_Count = 0 and high zero-month share (infrastructure-limited), (4) Cooler_Count = 0 and monthly volume below own Q90 / 3 (suppressed cold-storage outlet operating below capacity). Rule 4 was added in a second pass after we saw that 35% of outlets have zero coolers and many of those are clearly suppressed rather than fully constrained.

### Calibration and projection choices

- **Empirical seasonality calibration.** We decided to translate the categorical Seasonality_Index into per-distributor numeric multipliers from observed data rather than guess multipliers. Our reasoning: arbitrary numbers would be rubric-vulnerable; data-derived multipliers are defensible.
- **Year-over-year growth.** Geometric mean of Jan-over-Jan growth per distributor, clipped to [0.85, 1.30]. We chose geometric mean over arithmetic to dampen any single-year anomaly. We chose to clip rather than use raw values to prevent ratios from extreme low-volume years dominating the projection.
- **Jan 2026 holiday data.** Hand-curated from Sri Lankan government calendar: Duruthu Full Moon Poya (Jan 3) and Tamil Thai Pongal (Jan 14). We chose these dates ourselves; AI assisted only in formatting them into the schema the pipeline expected.
- **Sanity bounds.** Floor = Q95 of own history (robust to outlier months); ceiling = 5 × peer-cohort Q90. Choosing Q95 over `max()` was an explicit team correction after we realised one outlier month from a data-entry error would inflate the floor for the whole outlet.
- **Blend weights.** 0.40 peer-Q90 + 0.20 log-linear + 0.20 Tobit + 0.20 SFA when all four pairwise Spearman ρ exceed 0.75. Peer-Q90 gets anchor weight because it is the only non-parametric method; the three parametric methods share the rest equally. We chose 0.75 as the convergence threshold based on standard statistical practice for rank correlation strength.

### Validation strategy (no held-out y)

- **Internal-consistency design.** Four checks we authored: quantile sensitivity (Q85 / Q90 / Q95 overlap), constraint-rate plausibility (15-40% expected), magnitude ratio (predicted / observed mean), spatial Spearman (predicted vs POI density). These compensate for the absence of a held-out y by validating internal stability instead.
- **Hold-out validation idea.** Predict Jan 2025 from 2023+2024 only, compare to actual Jan 2025 unconstrained values. Spearman ρ = 0.805 was the result. This was the team's idea to convert "no y" into "limited y from the past" without changing the substantive prediction.
- **Constraint-threshold sensitivity.** We chose to audit how the suppressed-outlet count changes at 25 / 30 / 40 / 50% thresholds — showing the conclusions are not sensitive to the specific cut-off.

### Forensics, data hygiene and recovery

- **Reading the codebook xlsx.** We noticed that the codebook references a `Product_Name` column that does not exist in the raw transactions file, and that the seasonality filename differs (codebook says `distributor_seasonality.csv`, raw file is `distributor_seasonality_details.csv`). Both governance findings logged.
- **Outlet_Type normalisation map.** We curated the typo → canonical mapping (Grocry → Grocery, Bakry → Bakery, etc.) from raw value_counts. The Bakry → Bakery entry came only on the second EDA pass when we noticed Bakery existed as a canonical category and Bakry was a 395-outlet typo of it.
- **Outlet_Size case normalisation.** 600 outlets had `small` (lowercase). We chose to normalise to the canonical title-case `Small` so cohort grouping works correctly.
- **Lat/Lon swap recovery.** During EDA we noticed that some 'out-of-bbox' coordinates were actually Sri Lankan values with Latitude and Longitude transposed. We designed the swap-when-(lat∈lon-range AND lon∈lat-range) heuristic and recovered 200 outlets that the naive bbox quarantine would have dropped.
- **Negative-value 3-way classification.** We chose to keep return transactions (negative volume + negative bill) as signal that subtracts from monthly net rather than quarantining them — preserves 4,611 valid return rows that a simpler `≥ 0` rule would have destroyed.

### POI and feature engineering choices

- **POI tag selection.** 36 tag combinations grouped into 9 semantic categories (footfall, school, tourist, health, competitor_poi, worship, food_service, leisure_rec, population). We added cafe, fast_food, food_court, ice_cream specifically because beverage-consumption venues are the most direct catchment signal. We added landuse=residential as a population catchment proxy.
- **Distance-decay design.** Gaussian decay (`exp(-d²/2σ²)`) for the 8 demand-driver categories, gravity decay (`1/(d²+1)`) for competitor POIs. We tuned per-category sigma from domain reasoning: 150 m for food-service (a café across the road matters; one 1 km away does not), 1 km for tourism (hotel districts have a 1–2 km influence zone), 600 m for residential population (typical Sri Lankan walking-catchment).
- **POI ring radii (legacy flat counts).** We kept the original 500m / 1km / 2km / 5km flat counts alongside the new decay scores so the model has both representations; downstream feature engineering uses whichever fits the use case.
- **SKU mix features.** We classified the 10 SKUs into four price tiers (mass / mid / premium / super-premium) from observed price-per-litre statistics, then added avg_price_per_liter, premium_share, mid_share, mass_share, super_premium_share, sku_diversity, top_sku_share as features. This idea came from FMCG-domain reasoning that an outlet's premium-mix shape signals its consumer base.
- **Competitor density.** Per outlet: counts within 500m / 1km / 2km plus same-type subset. On top of that, we added the non-linear market-structure features (HHI over 1.5 km, Voronoi-style territory radius, type-weighted competition pressure with explicit similarity weights such as Grocery×Grocery=1.0 vs Grocery×Pharmacy=0.3).
- **Climate features.** Per-province January temperature and humidity averages curated by the team from Sri Lanka Department of Meteorology norms. Beverages are climate-sensitive; hotter / drier zones drive higher consumption.
- **Replenishment friction.** `stockout_flag_sum / active_months` with a top-quartile flag — our representation of the "cooler replenishment cycle" constraint the brief asks about. High values mean delivery / refill is failing the outlet even when nominal cooler capacity exists.

### Budget optimisation and channel decomposition

- **Maths choice.** Concave utility `Σ κ_i √(x_i)` reflects diminishing returns on promotional spend, a standard marketing-mix-modelling shape (Hanssens, Parsons & Schultz 2001; Jin et al 2017). We chose closed-form water-filling over a generic LP because it is fast, deterministic, and interpretable.
- **κ design.** `κ_i = uplift_i × cooler_priority_i × (1 + spatial_lift_i) / (1 + competitive_drag_i)` — every component is one the team can justify to a sales-rep audience.
- **Channel-weight heuristics.** Discount / Merchandising / Promotional shares per outlet condition (no-cooler / high-competition / low-spatial / default) come from team reasoning, not data. We documented this as a limitation and report a sensitivity sweep across plausible elasticity values rather than claiming a single calibrated liters number.
- **Per-outlet cap.** LKR 5,000 to prevent concentration; we verified the LP still uses ~99.5% of the budget with that cap in place.

### Business-decision modules (Round 2)

- **Cooler ROI.** LKR 50,000 subsidised cooler, 5-year service life, 12% gross margin, 12% cost of capital — all team-curated FMCG assumptions. We added the `is_material_case` filter (monthly uplift ≥ 5 L) so the Top-100 list is operationally meaningful.
- **Outlet action cards.** Four candidate interventions (add cooler / merchandising / SKU-mix shift / festival promo). The Sri Lankan festival calendar lifts (Avurudu 18%, Vesak 10%, Esala 12%, year-end 10%, Christmas 20%) were team-curated from cultural knowledge.
- **Dormancy classifier.** Label derivation (≥6 consecutive zero months, then resumed) and the feature whitelist that excludes `max_consecutive_zeros` and `zero_months_share` to avoid trivial label leakage — both team decisions.
- **Distributor scorecard.** Eight dimensions, weights team-chosen (15% coverage, 15% volume, 15% predicted potential, 15% low-risk share, 10% each for the remaining four).
- **Territories (HDBSCAN).** Tuning parameters (min_cluster_size=60, leaf cluster selection) and the "reassign noise to nearest centroid" step come from the team's read of the dataset density — default parameters produced 4 macro-clusters which were useless for sales planning.

### Web app and explainability

- **Architecture.** Mono-repo with FastAPI backend + Next.js frontend was a team decision driven by what already worked in Round 1's Python pipeline plus the need for a polished, demoable UI in Round 2.
- **Tabbed Insights workspace.** Combining six analytical views under a single `/insights` URL with search-param tabs was a team UX call; faster than per-route loads, deep-linkable, single sticky filter bar.
- **Gemini system prompt.** Team-written. Forbids inventing numbers, forbids statistical jargon, prescribes a "2 paragraphs + 3 bullets" output shape, sets temperature 0.4 for deterministic-style output. The LLM is positioned as a *translator* of the structured payload, not an inference engine.
- **XAI grounding.** Per-outlet payload contains only the structured factual substrate (predicted score, top-5 ± SHAP drivers, two counterfactual deltas, top-3 ranked actions, KPI summary). The raw historical volume is never sent — Gemini cannot hallucinate beyond what the pipeline already computed.

### Validation strategy (no held-out y)

- **Internal-consistency design.** Four checks we authored: quantile sensitivity (Q85 / Q90 / Q95 overlap), constraint-rate plausibility (15-40% expected), magnitude ratio (predicted / observed mean), spatial Spearman (predicted vs POI density). These compensate for the absence of a held-out y by validating internal stability instead.
- **Hold-out validation idea.** Predict Jan 2025 from 2023+2024 only, compare to actual Jan 2025 unconstrained values. Spearman ρ = 0.79 over ~11,200 outlets is the result. This was the team's idea to convert "no y" into "limited y from the past" without changing the substantive prediction.
- **Surrogate fidelity sanity.** Reported (Spearman ≈ 0.97, R² ≈ 0.998 vs ensemble) so reviewers can see the XGBoost surrogate is faithful enough that SHAP attributions are meaningful.
- **Constraint-threshold sensitivity.** Audit how the suppressed-outlet count changes at 25 / 30 / 40 / 50% thresholds — showing the conclusions are not sensitive to the specific cut-off.

### Repository, presentation and integrity

- **Repository hygiene.** Decisions to drop absolute paths from the bronze manifest, to add the env-var override, to commit audit artifacts but not raw competition data, to make the README a clean entry point, and to gitignore `.env` so the Gemini key never reaches the repo — all team-driven.
- **PDF structure.** Round-1 5-page PDF matched the four mandatory rubric sections (a/b/c/d) verbatim, with a Sri Lanka map, per-province bar chart, and Top-100 outlet ROI list as visual differentiators. Round-2 10-page paper extends this with the spend-optimisation logic and a dedicated GenAI section.
- **Business recommendation deliverables.** Five user-facing modules (Cooler ROI, Action cards, Dormancy risk, Scorecard, Territories) and a web app that surfaces all of them. Each module produces both a CSV audit artifact and a webapp tab so the output is consumable by both technical and non-technical audiences.
- **Honest limitations.** Constraint rule (ii) circularity, OSM rural coverage bias, weak spatial Spearman, no calibrated elasticity for the budget LP, counterfactuals are model-predicted (not empirically validated), channel split is heuristic — all surfaced in the methodology overview rather than hidden.

The AI's role across all of the above was implementation acceleration on team-specified designs, not design itself.

**Where we critically corrected AI:**
- Holiday primary key (Date alone vs Date + Holiday_Type) — would have wrongly quarantined 98.85% of holidays.
- Outlet_Type typo count (5,958 reported vs 979 actual) — misleading count fixed before report write.
- Sanity floor (max vs Q95 of own history) — a single outlier month would have inflated potential for entire outlet.
- Phase-3 historical_peak — initial fallback order put `max()` first; we reversed it so Q95 is the primary anchor.
- Negative-bill blanket quarantine rule — replaced with 3-way classification that preserved 4,611 return signals.
- Hardcoded local raw-data path — replaced with env-var override + dropped absolute paths from manifest.
- Incomplete typo map (Bakry missed) — second EDA pass caught 4 outlet types we hadn't normalised (Bakry, Bakery, Kiosk, SMMT); fixed and re-ran.
- 7 POI tags were missing from the distance-decay categories after the first pass (notably `landuse=residential` as a catchment proxy). Added two new categories (`leisure_rec`, `population`) and merged the rest into existing buckets.
- Composite `spatial_demand_score` initially weighted only 5 of 9 POI categories; team added worship + food_pairing + leisure_rec + population so the signal reflects beverage-relevant demand drivers.
- Counterfactuals: surrogate model first produced *negative* delta for "add a cooler" on some outlets (model artefact). Team added monotonic constraints (Cooler_Count: +1, competitor_density_norm: -1, has_no_cooler: -1, replenishment_friction: -1) so the directions match domain intuition.
- Gemini 2.5 Flash output was being truncated to ~60 chars because thinking tokens consume `max_output_tokens`; team raised the budget to 4096 and added a fallback that reads from `response.candidates[]` when `.text` errors out.
- AI narrative was rendering as raw markdown (`**bold**`, `* bullets`) in the UI; team added `react-markdown` + `remark-gfm` with explicit Tailwind component mappings.
- HDBSCAN first pass at default parameters produced 4 macro-clusters (one per province), too coarse for sales territory planning. Team tuned to `min_cluster_size=60, leaf` and added a "reassign noise to nearest centroid" step so every outlet has a territory id, yielding 96 useful sub-province sales territories.
- Holiday multiplier counted `nunique(Date)` on one side and `groupby.size()` on the other; mismatch produced a uniform 3.6% under-prediction. Team fixed both sides to `nunique(Date)`.
- SFA optimiser hit `maxiter` limit at the default 200 / 300 iterations; team raised to 500 so it converges cleanly without warnings.
- Pandas 3.0 `groupby.apply` silently dropped the Province column during POI density tier assignment; team replaced the apply pattern with an in-place mutation using `groupby.groups.items()`.
- AI narrative payload first leaked all 229 features into the prompt; team trimmed to the 10 most relevant signals so the system prompt stays focused and the LLM cannot invent metrics.
- Outlets table initially used `table-auto` so column widths shifted whenever filters changed the visible data; team switched to `table-fixed` with percentage widths so the columns are pinned and only the cell *contents* swap when a filter runs.
